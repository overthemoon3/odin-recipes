Fried Noodles (Grandma's Version)

Home Page

Description

This dish has quite a special place in my heart. Back when I used to visit my grandparents in India, their house was located right by some food trucks. So, every evening, the scent of delicious Indian street food used to waft over to our house. My mom, worried for my weak American immune system, never let me eat the street food there, so on special occasions, my grandmother used to make the same kind of food right at home! This style of fried noodles (called Hakka noodbles) is very popular, and everytime I eat it, I'm reminded of lazy summer evenings and good company.

This summer, I had the privillege of bringing my grandparents to America for the first time, and while here, my grandma taught me the recipe!

Ingredients

  • noodles (preferably made from semolina)
  • bell peppers
  • cabbage
  • shreaded carrots
  • chopped onion
  • spring onion
  • ginger chilli paste
  • chilli vinegar
  • soy sauce
  • chings schezwan chutney (any kind of indochinese chutney will do, this is just what she uses)

Steps

  1. preboil the noodles, strain and run cold water through them to separate them out
  2. saute onion, chilli garlic paste, and chopped bell peppers in a separate pan
  3. add chopped cabbage and carrot and continue to cook for 10 more minutes
  4. finally add the noodles and start gently mixing in the chilli vinegar, soy sauce, and schezwan chutney
  5. top off with chopped spring onion, and you're done!
